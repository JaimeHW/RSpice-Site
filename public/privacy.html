PRIVACY / CURRENT SERVICE STATUS

Your circuit does not travel through this site.

This notice describes the public RSpice website and voluntary communications today. Account, cloud-project, collaboration, managed-compute, billing, and publishing services are planned—not live.

01 / TODAY

What this site handles.

The current website is static. RSpice may receive the information you intentionally send in an email—such as a sales inquiry, feedback, support request, or attached diagnostic material—and ordinary technical request data that browsers and hosting infrastructure use to deliver and secure a website.

RSpice does not sell personal information or use this website for cross-context behavioral advertising.

02 / LOCAL WORK

Local stays local.

No account is required for current local workflows.Building or running the source tools does not upload a circuit, model, result, or deck through this marketing site.

Do not email confidential circuit or model files unless an appropriate protected channel has been established. Keep independent copies of critical work and evaluate development software against your own engineering requirements.

03 / BEFORE CONNECTED SERVICES

The future service will need a real policy.

RSpice intends to offer identity, project synchronization, live review, managed simulation, and publishable result pages. Those services are not available yet, and this page is not a substitute for their production privacy notice, terms, security architecture, retention schedule, or data-processing agreement.

Before a connected service launches, RSpice will publish service-specific documentation that describes live data flows, access controls, hosting and subprocessors, retention and deletion, payment handling, sharing visibility, security reporting, and applicable user rights. That material requires appropriate legal review for the jurisdictions in which the service is offered.

04 / CONTACT

Questions and updates.

For a website privacy question, contact privacy@rspice.app. For a suspected security issue, use the repository’s private security reporting channel.

This service-status notice is effective July 10, 2026 and will be replaced or expanded before RSpice activates accounts, payments, cloud storage, collaboration, managed compute, or publishing.
